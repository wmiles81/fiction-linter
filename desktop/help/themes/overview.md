## Theme Overview

Fiction Linter ships with four built-in themes designed for different writing environments and personal preferences. The theme changes the editor's background, text color, and UI chrome — it does not affect your document content or findings.

### How to switch themes

Click the **theme picker dropdown** in the **top-right corner** of the window (it shows the name of the current theme). Select any theme from the list and the interface updates immediately — no restart required.

You can also right-click the theme picker to see a mini-popup with a link to this help topic.

### The four themes

| Theme | Best for |
|-------|---------|
| **Parchment** | Daytime writing, warm natural light, the default |
| **Midnight** | Late-night sessions, low-light environments |
| **Sepia** | Long reading and revision passes, easy on the eyes |
| **High Contrast** | Maximum legibility, accessibility needs |

### Persistence

Your selected theme is saved automatically and restored the next time you open Fiction Linter. It applies to all documents and is stored per-device (not per-project).

### Findings overlay colors

The red/orange/blue severity underlines remain consistent and clearly visible across all four themes. The High Contrast theme uses the most distinct underline styles for users who need maximum differentiation.
