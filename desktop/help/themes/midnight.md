## Midnight

**Midnight** is Fiction Linter's dark theme. It swaps the warm cream background for a deep navy surface and renders body text in soft amber — a combination that minimizes eye strain when writing in dim or dark environments.

### Design intent

Midnight is tuned for late-night sessions, writing in bed, or any situation where a bright white or cream screen would be uncomfortable. The navy-and-amber palette is warmer than a standard dark-gray-on-white dark mode, making it easier to read prose for long periods.

### Colors at a glance

- **Editor background** — deep navy blue
- **Body text** — warm amber / pale gold
- **Accents and active elements** — bright amber or pale yellow
- **Toolbar and UI chrome** — slightly lighter navy with low-contrast borders

### Findings underlines on Midnight

All three severity underlines (red, orange, blue) are adjusted for visibility on the dark background. Red and orange use slightly brighter tones than in the light themes. Blue info findings appear as a soft sky blue that is easy to distinguish from the amber text.

### Tips for Midnight

- If you use an external monitor, consider your room's ambient light. Midnight works best when the room is dim; a very bright room may cause the dark editor to feel like a "hole" in your visual field.
- The **High Contrast** theme is a better choice if you need maximum legibility in a dark environment.

### Switching themes

Click the **theme picker dropdown** in the **top-right corner** of the window.
