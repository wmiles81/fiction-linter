## High Contrast

**High Contrast** is Fiction Linter's accessibility theme. It uses a pure white background, near-black body text, and vivid blue accents to achieve maximum legibility. If you have low vision, contrast sensitivity, or simply prefer the crispest possible reading surface, this theme is designed for you.

### Design intent

High Contrast aims to meet WCAG AA contrast standards for all text and interactive elements. Unlike the warmer themes, no design comfort has been traded for aesthetics — every color choice prioritizes readability first.

### Colors at a glance

- **Editor background** — pure white
- **Body text** — near-black (#111 or equivalent)
- **Accents and active elements** — vivid blue
- **Toolbar and UI chrome** — white with clear, dark borders

### Findings underlines on High Contrast

The three severity underlines use their most distinct variants in this theme:

- **Red errors** — bold, saturated red
- **Orange warnings** — strong orange with no warmth blending into the background
- **Blue info** — deep blue with a wavy underline style to distinguish from the UI accent blue

### When to use High Contrast

- You use screen magnification software
- You find the warmer themes too low-contrast for comfortable extended reading
- You are printing a findings review and want maximum ink contrast
- You're sharing your screen and need findings to be visible to others

### Switching themes

Click the **theme picker dropdown** in the **top-right corner** of the window.
