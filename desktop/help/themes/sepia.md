## Sepia

**Sepia** is a warm, mid-toned theme inspired by the yellowed pages of an old paperback. The background sits between Parchment's brighter cream and Midnight's deep navy — making it a comfortable middle ground for extended reading and revision work.

### Design intent

Sepia is built for revision passes rather than first drafts. When you're reading through a completed chapter and evaluating findings rather than generating new text, a slightly darker, more muted background reduces the visual "noise" of a bright surface. The burgundy accents add a sense of editorial seriousness without going full dark mode.

### Colors at a glance

- **Editor background** — aged tan / light brown
- **Body text** — dark brown, nearly black
- **Accents and active elements** — burgundy / deep red
- **Toolbar and UI chrome** — medium tan with warm-toned borders

### Findings underlines on Sepia

Red error underlines stand out clearly against the tan background. Orange warnings blend slightly more with the warm tones — if you work primarily with warning-level findings, High Contrast offers stronger orange differentiation. Blue info underlines are clearly visible as a contrasting cooler tone.

### Switching themes

Click the **theme picker dropdown** in the **top-right corner** of the window.
