## Line Editing Protocol

The **Line Editing Protocol** (`line_editing_protocol.yaml`) is the editorial rulebook that guides AI-powered rewrites — **Fix Now** operations and the broader **AI Scan**. It is not primarily a pattern-matching file like the other three rule sets; it is a set of principles the AI is instructed to follow.

### Core principles

**Concreteness and embodiment** — Replace abstract phrasing with concrete sensory detail. "She felt a wave of sadness" becomes something physical: a tightened throat, a thumbnail pressed into a palm. Abstract emotional labels are replaced with specific physical reactions, behaviors, or micro-observations.

**Character-driven language** — Adjust diction to fit the point-of-view character's background, age, mood, and personality. Business-casual, sanitized, or generic wording that does not match the character's voice is removed. Each character should sound like themselves, not like an AI's idea of a neutral narrator.

**Subtext and restraint** — Remove lines that explain what is already implied by action or context. Trust the reader. "He slammed the door, showing just how angry he was" becomes "He slammed the door."

**Dialogue authenticity** — Dialogue should sound real, not scripted. Polite, balanced, symmetrical phrasing is replaced with interruptions, fragments, evasions, and emotional friction. Grammar follows the speaker, not a style guide.

**Sentence rhythm** — Vary sentence length to reflect emotional pacing: sharp and short in tension, longer and flowing in introspection. Remove filler sentences that do not move the scene.

### What it does not change

The protocol explicitly prohibits altering plot, character motivations, or scene outcomes. It also preserves "somatic-conceptual fusion" (e.g., "smells like bleach and existential dread") in dialogue, where it signals character voice — while banning the same construction in authorial narration.
